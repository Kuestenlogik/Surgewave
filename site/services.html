How an engagement works

1. Discovery call (free, 30 minutes)

We listen first. What is Surgewave doing for you, what is it about to do, and where is the risk? You leave the call with a written one-pager: scope, timeline, fixed-price or T&M shape, and a clear next step. No NDA pressure on this call — bring the architecture, leave the secrets at home.

2. Statement of work

Within five working days you have a signed-and-scoped SoW. Production support runs on a yearly retainer with a documented SLA. Custom plugins and reviews are fixed-price per deliverable. Migration & training engagements scale by cluster size and team count — ranges are public, no quotation lottery.

3. Delivery

We work in your repos and your incident-response channels. Every commit gets a postcard explanation in the PR description. Every architectural decision lands in an ADR you keep. When the engagement ends, your team owns everything — no consultancy lock-in, no opaque scripts, no "secret" plugins your engineers can't read.

4. Handover & tail

Two weeks of free post-engagement office hours after every fixed-price delivery so your team can ask the silly questions that always come up once the new code hits production. Anything beyond that rolls into the regular support retainer or stays one-off, your choice.

What we say no to

We don't take engagements where Surgewave isn't the right tool. If you should be running NATS, Redis Streams, or even plain Postgres LISTEN/NOTIFY, we'll tell you on the discovery call — usually in writing so you can take it to your leadership. Honesty here is cheaper than a six-month "Surgewave rollout" we both regret.

Who you'll work with

The same engineers who write the broker. No bench rotations, no offshoring the actual work. Küstenlogik is small on purpose; the people on the call are the people on the keyboard.
